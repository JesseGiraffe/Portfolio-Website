The Problem

As part of the Product Design III capstone course, we worked with outside entrepreneurs to bring their ideas to life, developing them from TRL1 to TRL3. My group was tasked with developing a fun, compact, and easy-to-use action camera system. Unlike a smartphone, it has been designed to blend seamlessly into any activity. allowing end users to capture and share every moment effortlessly, bringing the focus away from lining up the perfect shot and back to the action at hand. The overall goal of this project was to develop a functional proof-of-concept prototype for our entrepreneur, along with the corresponding documentation and proposed look-alike models.

The Process

The first step in our process was to establish our design requirements. This involved several meetings and interviews with our external entrepreneur to determine the project direction. Once our design requirements were determined, we began our ideation phase. This involved developing concept sketches of our look models and creating flow charts to determine how our electronic systems and components would interact. Once ideation was completed, we produced a bill of materials to present all our required components and costs to our entrepreneur. Once the costs were approved and the parts ordered, we moved on to the construction phase. We are currently working to develop several iterations of functional prototypes with different configurations.
