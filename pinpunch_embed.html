Pinpunch (Lathe Project)

The Problem

For this project, I was tasked with producing a pin punch tool using a Prototrak Lathe. Through careful selection of tooling, machining operation order, and feed and speed rates, I was able to produce a tool meeting all required project specifications.

The Process

Once the brass stock had been selected and cut down to size, I first began using a center drill to produce a hole on either end so the part could be placed on a live center in either direction. Next, I added the required knurling for the design, conducting several passes. After the knurling was added, I began machining the pin end of the punch down to spec, lowering the machine’s speed and feed rates for a clean final pass. I then flipped the part, allowing me to cut the required radius and chamfer on the other end of the punch. After my final pass, I moved over to the mill, placed the part into a pair of soft jaws, and cut a slot to the desired depth where my initials could be stamped.

The Results

In the end, I was left with a pin punch tool meeting all required design specifications, as shown in the photos to the left of this document. This project demonstrates my ability to produce parts successfully with a lathe by proving I can manufacture parts that meet all design and functional requirements and tolerances.
